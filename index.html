May Monthly Market Snapshot

📈 S&P 500 Posts 9 Straight Winning Weeks

May extended the historic rally with the S&P 500 gaining +5%, the Nasdaq +8% — its best month of 2026 — and the Dow crossing 51,000 for the first time ever, gaining +3%. Markets strung together nine consecutive winning weeks, the longest streak since 2023, driven by continued AI momentum and strong earnings from tech heavyweights.

🤖 NVDA Delivers Record $81.6B Quarter

NVIDIA reported May 20 with record revenue of $81.6B (+85% YoY), beating estimates of $78.8B. Adjusted EPS of $1.87 beat the $1.76 consensus. Data center revenue reached $75.2B — nearly doubling year-over-year and accounting for 92% of total sales. CEO Jensen Huang called Blackwell demand "off the charts." Despite the massive beat, shares dipped slightly on buy-the-rumor dynamics.

📊 Hot CPI Revives Rate Hike Fears

April CPI came in at +3.8% YoY — the highest since May 2023 — shocking markets and sending rate hike odds from near zero to 45% in a single week. PCE rose to 3.8% as of month-end, the highest reading in nearly three years. Kevin Warsh was sworn in as the new Fed Chair on May 16, replacing Jerome Powell. His first rate decision comes at the June 16–17 FOMC meeting — the most watched policy event of the year.

☮️ Iran 60-Day Ceasefire Extended

The U.S. and Iran agreed to a 60-day memorandum extending the ceasefire and allowing unrestricted Strait of Hormuz traffic for negotiations on Iran's nuclear program. Oil retreated from above $105 toward the low-$90s on the news, providing some inflation relief. However, intermittent strikes and unresolved terms kept markets cautious, with Goldman Sachs forecasting Brent averaging $90/barrel in Q4 2026.

Federal Reserve Update

The Fed held rates steady at 3.50%–3.75% as sticky inflation complicated the path to cuts. Kevin Warsh remains on track to replace Jerome Powell as Fed Chair in May.

⚠ Tariff & Trade Policy Uncertainty

The Supreme Court struck down emergency universal tariffs as exceeding executive authority. President Trump's State of the Union reaffirmed commitment to tariffs, setting up continued volatility.

Sector & Asset Class Highlights

Growth Reclaims Leadership

The April-May rally reversed the year's value/growth divergence. Russell 1000 Growth surged back to roughly flat YTD as AI and tech dominated, while Russell 1000 Value held steady at +6% YTD. The Nasdaq's +8% May was its best month of 2026, led by semiconductors, software, and cloud computing.

Oil Volatile: $90–$107 Range in May

Crude oil traded in a wide $90–$107 range as ceasefire news and Iran tensions alternated in driving sentiment. Goldman Sachs forecasts Brent averaging $90/barrel in Q4. Energy stocks gave back some March gains but remain one of the top-performing sectors YTD at +18.5%.

AI Infrastructure Dominates Capex

Hyperscalers continued raising AI capex with Meta guiding $125–$145B and Microsoft $190B for 2026. Dell and Snowflake each surged over 40% in the final week of May on AI infrastructure demand. The AI buildout theme remains the single most powerful earnings driver of 2026.

Equities

Slightly Underweight

Market Overview

The U.S. economy continued to advance in 2025 despite the labor market providing a contrasting signal of growing weakness. Equity markets remained resilient and pushed markedly higher with Large-Cap stocks posting their third straight year of 15+% returns.

Market Dynamics

Over the past few years, both the economy and markets have narrowed driven by the bifurcated impact of the Fed's tightening cycle on individual consumers and businesses. The AI boom and K-shaped consumer have propelled growth for the economy and markets the last few years.

Investment Strategy

We continue to believe that the delicate balance in both the economy and markets will give way to a broader advance in the coming years. We remain focused on the intermediate to long-term while positioning for lower, but broader equity returns in 2026.

Current Positioning

Favor: U.S. Small-Caps and Mid-Caps reflecting improving earnings growth prospects and attractive relative valuations

Tilt: Equal-weighted versus market cap-weighted S&P given concerns about valuations and concentration

Underweight: Real Estate equity, which is sensitive to interest rates and pressured rent growth conditions

Fixed Income

Overweight

Strategic Shift

We were tactically underweight fixed income for much of the past seven years leading up to 2023 as the asset class offered little expected return over the anticipated rate of inflation. Our positioning moved to overweight in 2024 as the dramatic repricing vastly improved the real return potential and diversification benefits within our portfolios.

2025 Performance Validation

2025 performance was a testament to this improved risk/return relationship as the asset class posted a 7+% return with just a 2.5% drawdown in the second quarter.

Diversification Benefit

When recession fears spiked in 2024 and 2025, bonds appeared to hedge equity downside, confirming their renewed role as portfolio ballast.

Real Assets

Underweight

Current Positioning Rationale

Portfolio Context

Private Equity — Asset Class Overview

Private equity has historically delivered a meaningful return premium over public markets across full market cycles, driven by the illiquidity premium, operational value creation, and access to company growth at stages unavailable in public markets. The chart below reflects the Private Equity Index Total Return (^PEATR) — a broad market benchmark providing a transparent, market-level view of PE performance relative to public equities.

📈 Rationale

PE captures an illiquidity premium by investing in companies not subject to daily public market pricing, historically generating excess returns across full cycles. A multi-strategy approach spanning buyouts, growth equity, and secondaries reduces vintage-year concentration risk and smooths the J-curve.

🎯 Target Client

Best suited for clients with long investment horizons and limited near-term liquidity requirements. Evergreen and interval fund structures have made PE more accessible — offering periodic subscriptions and tender offers rather than fully locked capital.

🏗️ Portfolio Fit

PE is typically allocated as a return enhancer within the equity sleeve, targeting long-term capital appreciation beyond what public markets can offer. Manager selection is critical — dispersion between top- and bottom-quartile PE managers is significantly wider than in public equity markets.

Private Credit — Asset Class Overview

Private credit — primarily direct lending — has emerged as one of the fastest-growing segments of the alternative investment landscape, offering floating-rate income, reduced correlation to public bond markets, and a yield premium driven by the complexity and illiquidity of non-bank lending. The VanEck BDC Income ETF (BIZD) tracks the MVIS U.S. Business Development Companies Index — the broadest, most liquid publicly available proxy for the direct lending market.

📈 Rationale

Private credit generates attractive income through floating-rate loans that naturally adjust upward as rates rise, providing an inherent hedge against rate risk that traditional fixed income lacks. Non-bank lenders fill a gap created by tightened bank lending standards post-2008.

🎯 Target Client

Ideal for income-oriented clients seeking yield enhancement above traditional fixed income who are willing to accept reduced liquidity in exchange for a significant premium.

$2M+ Accredited Investor$5M+ Qualified Purchaser
🏗️ Portfolio Fit

Private credit fits within the fixed income or alternative income sleeve, serving as a yield enhancer relative to investment grade bonds. Senior-secured positioning and floating rate structures provide dual downside protection.
